Two notes worth knowing.
(1) Every Showcase was renamed mid-stream. All 7 events were originally titled with their partner Speed Lab's name (e.g., Transcend, Veritas, Hansen) and rebranded to "USR Showcase: [State]" between April 28 and May 7, 2026. Scope is event_id throughout — registrations and CTA clicks from both name eras are included for each event.
(2) Attribution method. The web pixel doesn't capture user_id on page views (every visitor is anonymous), and server-side registration_completed doesn't carry UTMs. Mid-funnel events attribute cleanly via anonymous_id. Paid registrations use a temporal-proximity bridge — find the closest CTA/Reg-Start event by anonymous_id within ±15 min, then trace its page-view UTMs. Worth a ticket to engineering to plumb user_id through page-view tracking.

Methodology. Page views come from website_prod.pages filtered to path LIKE '/events/{id}%'. CTA Click, Reg Started, Reg Completed, and Payment Submitted are filtered by event_id={id} (cast to varchar) in their respective tables — this covers both original partner-named eras and the renamed "USR Showcase: [State]" eras. Mid-funnel attribution joins pages.anonymous_id to the union of CTA + Reg-Started anonymous_ids — reliable. Paid-reg attribution bridges registration_completed.user_id → nearest anonymous_id on CTA/Reg-Started within ±15 min, then back to pages — inferred. Revenue is shown in dollars (Stripe amounts are stored in cents).
